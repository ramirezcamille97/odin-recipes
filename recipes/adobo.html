Adobo

Description

Pork Adobo made with succulent pork belly braised in vinegar, soy sauce, garlic, and onions. A delicious balance of salty and savory, this hearty stew is Philippineâ€™s national dish for a good reason!

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs porkbelly
  • 1 onion, peeled and sliced thinly
  • 1 head garlic, peeled and minced
  • 2 bay leaves
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon pepper
  • 1 tablespoon oil
  • 1 cup vinegar
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1 cup water

Instructions

  1. In a bowl, combine pork, onions, garlic, bay leaves, salt, and pepper. Place in the refrigerator and marinate for about 30 minutes
  2. In a wide, heavy-bottomed skillet over medium heat, heat oil. Add meat mixture and cook, turning occasionally, until pork is lightly browned
  3. Add the pork belly and cook until outer part turns light brown
  4. Add vinegar and allow to boil, uncovered and without stirring, for about 3 to 5 minutes
  5. Add soy sauce and water and stir to combine. Allow to a boil for another 3 to 5 minutes
  6. Lower heat, cover, and simmer for about 40 to 50 minutes or until meat is fork-tender and sauce is reduced. Serve hot
